About Us

SOS Multistate Credit Co-operative Bank strictly abides by the Multistate Co-operative Societies Act 2002 and is doing finance business among its members.

Society is not a personal institution owned by an individual but is a fully democratic organization managed by the 'Board of Directors' who are elected by the members of the society in the Annual General Meeting and the Board of Directors are responsible to take decisions unanimously with total transparency. In order to give better service to the community all our branches render all various types of services through its fully computerized offices.

This society has a wide shareholders base, because its members come from all walks of life i.e. self employed, professionals, salaried persons, small industrialists, traders, farmers, senior citizens and prominent individuals in society. All the deposit & loan schemes have always attracted customers to be a part of the organization. Since our Society is managed by highly professional and experienced peoples, we are capable and committed to provide reasonable and practically achievable interest rate on deposited amount for sure and steady growth of its members and share holders.

Initially, our operation started in Latur, which is our Head Office & further expansion to Nasik, Ahmednagar, Aurangabad, Dhule district is ongoing and more than ten branches have already started their normal operations in their respective area. Our next expansion will be in rest of Maharashtra. Since our expansion programme is on a fast track, we are also committed to have our operations in many other states and throughout the country after permission obtaind by central government.

  • Voluntary and Open Membership

    SOS Bank is voluntary organization, open to all persons able to use their services and willing to accept the responsibilities of membership, without gender, social, racial, political or religious discrimination.

  • Democratic Member Control

    SOS bank is democratic organization controlled by their members, who actively participate in setting their policies and making decisions. Men and women serving as elected representatives are accountable to the membership. In primary co-operatives members have equal voting rights (one member, one vote).

  • Member Economic Participation

    Members contribute equitably to, and democratically control, the capital of their co-operative. At least part of that capital is usually the common property of the co-operative. Members usually receive limited compensation, if any, on capital subscribed as a condition of membership. Members allocate surpluses for any or all of the following purposes: developing their co-operative, possibly by setting up reserves, part of which at least would be indivisible; benefiting members in proportion to their transactions with the co-operative; and supporting other activities approved by the membership.

  • Autonomy and Independence

    SOS Bank is autonomous, self-help organizations controlled by their members.
