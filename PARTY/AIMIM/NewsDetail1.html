NCP's vote bank under threat due to entry of AIMIM in PCMC

PIMPRI: The maiden entry of All India Majlis-e-ittehad-ul Muslimeen (AIMIM) in Pimpri-Chinchwad politics is a wake-up call for the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP), which is the ruling party in Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC).

AIMIM's focus is to win over the Muslim and Dalit voters of city, which is the traditional vote bank of the NCP.

But NCP's main concern is polarisation of voters that can indirectly help saffron brigade in city.

The entry of AIMIM into Pimpri-Chinchwad political arena comes at a time when the NCP is facing internal squabbles. The civic polls are just 15 months away.

Akil Mujawar, new president of AIMIM Pimpri Chinchwad unit, said admitted that NCP enjoys support of the Muslim community.

"But issues of that community like education and inadequacy of burial ground etc are yet to be resolved. Our efforts will be to take up these issues so that the problems are resolved"

Akil Mujawar

AIMIM claimed that the opposition in PCMC is ineffective and the problems which people are facing are not getting proper attention and redressal.

According to Mujawar, AIMIM is going to conduct a survey of all wards from where its candidates can contest.

"We will accommodate candidates from all communities to widen its support base".

Akil Mujawar

AIMIM sources said that there are about 15 to 17 wards where the population of Muslim community is significant and the party is planning to contest civic polls from these wards

As per the 2011 census, the city has nearly one lakh population of Muslims. A large chunk of them comprises migrant workers from states like Uttar Pradesh, Bihar and West Bengal.

However, NCP leaders claimed that AIMIM will not make any dent into NCP's support base in that community .

"We don't see AIMIM as threat. We have connected well with all communities at the grass root level. Some of our prominent leaders are from the community and that we have faith in the community."

NCP city unit chief Sanjog Waghere
